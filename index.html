New Take Away Orders Process

Operation with Buzzer System (Syscall)

A step-by-step guide for proper service, counter decluttering, and maintaining our strictly Take Away character.

Why are we changing the process?

2 / 6

Solving 2 Main Problems:

  • No more searching for customers: Customers often wandered off or got "lost," and staff wasted valuable time trying to find them.
  • We are STRICTLY a Take Away shop: Staff does not carry food outside or to tables. Customers must come to the counter themselves for pickup.
Goal: Zero wasted time for staff, absolute customer autonomy!

Step 1: Order Taking & POS

3 / 6

System Registration:

  • Open a new order and select "Take away".
  • Before finalizing, press "Invoice Note".
  • Type the buzzer number in the "PAGER NUMBER" field on the POS.
  • Take the corresponding buzzer from the charging base and hand it to the customer along with the receipt.

Step 2: Customer Waiting

4 / 6

What does the customer do?

  • The customer can sit in the outdoor area or wait nearby without blocking the pickup counter.
  • We don't look for them: We know that when the time comes, they will be notified automatically.
  • The buzzer displays their number and has clear instructions on it: "When the beeper is vibrating, Please come to the pick-up desk."

Step 3: Preparation & Paging

5 / 6

How to call the customer:

  • As soon as the kitchen prepares the order and it is packed in the bag, check the Pager number from the printed ticket.
  • On the central Syscall console, type the number of the buzzer.
  • Press the CALL button.
Tip: Watch the video on the right to see the exact sequence of keys on the console!

Step 4: Pickup at the Counter

6 / 6

Order Completion:

  • The customer's buzzer starts vibrating, flashing red, and making a sound.
  • The customer returns to the pickup counter on their own.
  • Collect the buzzer from the customer and place it back on the charging base (to make it stop ringing).
  • Hand them their order.
REMEMBER: We do not take the food to the customer. Pickup is done exclusively by them at the counter!
